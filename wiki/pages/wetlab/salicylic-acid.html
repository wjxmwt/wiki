    Wet Lab · Experiments

    Construction of Salicylic Acid Sensing Pathway

    We engineered a whole-cell biosensor by reconstructing the NahR/Psal regulatory system to detect salicylic acid — the core intermediate in bacterial PAH degradation pathways.

    {% endblock %} {# ==================== 文章主体 ==================== #} {% block article %}

    Overview

    Research has shown that the degradation of most PAHs in bacteria proceeds through the salicylic acid pathway (Salicylic acid, SaA), ultimately entering the tricarboxylic acid cycle. Therefore, we chose salicylic acid as the inducer for our whole-cell biosensor to construct the salicylic acid sensing pathway.

    Materials & Methods

    PCR System & Program

    PCR System (50 μL)
    Reagent Volume
    2× Phanta Max Master Mix 25 μL
    Forward Primer (10 μM) 2 μL
    Reverse Primer (10 μM) 2 μL
    Template DNA 1 μL
    ddH₂O up to 50 μL
    PCR Program
    1. Pre-denaturation: 98 °C, 10 min — complete denaturation of template DNA and activation of hot-start DNA polymerase.
    2. Cycling (28 cycles): Denaturation: 98 °C, 30 s → Annealing: 60 °C, 30 s → Extension: 72 °C, 30 s.
    3. Final extension: 72 °C, 5 min — complete all truncated products.
    4. Hold at 12 °C to prevent product degradation.

    Gel Recovery Protocol

    DNA Gel Extraction
    1. After electrophoresis, excise the target DNA band on a UV transilluminator with a clean scalpel. Blot dry and trim excess gel, then dice into small pieces and transfer to an EP tube.
    2. Add 300 μL Buffer GDP; incubate at 55–60 °C in a water bath for ~10 min, inverting several times until gel is completely dissolved.
    3. Transfer dissolved gel to the column; centrifuge at 12 000 rpm, 30 s. Discard filtrate; repeat if liquid remains.
    4. Add 300 μL Buffer GDP to column; 12 000 rpm, 30 s.
    5. Add 650 μL Wash Buffer; 12 000 rpm, 1 min. Discard filtrate.
    6. Add 700 μL Buffer GW (with ethanol); 12 000 rpm, 30 s. Discard filtrate.
    7. Repeat previous step; then spin empty at 12 000 rpm, 2 min.
    8. Transfer column to a sterile 1.5 mL EP tube. Add 30 μL pre-warmed (65 °C) ddH₂O to the center. Let stand 2–5 min; 12 000 rpm, 1 min. Discard column, keep eluate.

    Competent Cell Transformation

    Heat-Shock Transformation
    1. Ice bath: Add 5 μL recombinant product to 100 μL thawed competent cells, gently mix, incubate on ice for 30 min.
    2. Heat shock: Place tube in 42 °C water bath for exactly 45 seconds.
    3. Recovery: Immediately transfer to ice, cool for 2 min. Add 200 μL pre-warmed antibiotic-free LB medium.
    4. Incubate at 37 °C, 180 rpm shaking for 45 min.
    5. Centrifuge at 5 000 rpm, 3 min. Remove supernatant (~200 μL). Resuspend pellet in ~100 μL medium; spread evenly onto LB + Kana plates.
    6. Once absorbed, invert plates and incubate at 37 °C overnight (12–16 h).

    Plasmid Extraction

    Mini-Prep Protocol
    1. Collect 2–5 mL culture into an EP tube; 12 000 rpm, 1 min; collect pellet.
    2. Add 250 μL Solution P1; vortex to resuspend.
    3. Add 250 μL Solution P2; gently invert to mix.
    4. Add 350 μL Solution P3 until white flocculent forms; gently invert; 12 000 rpm, 10 min.
    5. Carefully transfer supernatant to column (avoid pellet); 12 000 rpm, 1 min.
    6. Add 500 μL Solution PW1; 12 000 rpm, 30 s.
    7. Add 600 μL Solution PW2; 12 000 rpm, 30 s. Repeat once.
    8. Discard filtrate; spin empty 12 000 rpm, 3 min to remove residual liquid.
    9. Place column in new sterile 1.5 mL EP tube. Add 30–100 μL pre-warmed Elution Buffer; stand 2 min; 12 000 rpm, 2 min.
    10. Store DNA at −20 °C.

    1. NahR/Psal System

    Literature review reveals that the Psal promoter on plasmid NAH7 of Pseudomonas putida G7, together with its homologous regulatory gene nahR, controls the expression of two operons (nahAaAbAcAdBFCED and nahGTHINLOMKL) associated with naphthalene metabolism. The upper operon encodes enzymes converting naphthalene to salicylic acid, initiated by the Pnah promoter; the lower operon converts salicylic acid to TCA cycle intermediates, initiated by Psal. NahR responds to salicylic acid to activate transcription.

    Key Reference: Previous studies used the NahR/Psal system from Pseudomonas putida G7 NAH7 with Luc as reporter in E. coli DH5α. Site-directed mutagenesis of NahR (N169E/R248C) yielded lower basal activity and higher induction at 1 mM salicylic acid — providing the basis for our reconstructed biosensor.

    2. Plasmid Construction: pUC57-Psal-eGFP

    Primer Design & Gene Synthesis

    CDS sequences of Pr-nahR (E169/C248)-Psal and eGFP were retrieved from NCBI and sent for primer synthesis. Homology arms matching the vector were appended to primer termini for subsequent recombination.

    PCR & Linearization

    PCR-amplified Pr-nahR (E169/C248)-Psal and eGFP fragments (with homology arms); restriction-enzyme-linearized pUC57 vector (exposing matching homology arms at both ends).

    Product Verification

    5 μL PCR product was run on agarose gel to confirm a single band of correct size. Remaining PCR product was run and the target band excised for DNA purification.

    Homologous Recombination

    ClonExpress II Assembly (5 μL system)
    Component Volume
    5× CE II Buffer 1 μL
    Exnase II 1 μL
    Linearized fragment : Vector 2 : 1

    Mix evenly, then incubate at 37 °C for 30 min.

    After transformation, pick single colonies into 5 mL LB + antibiotics; shake at 37 °C, 220 rpm for 12 h, then send for sequencing.

    3. Sensor Validation — Detection Limit

    Experimental Controls

    Positive control: E. coli DH5α successfully transformed with pUC57-Psal-eGFP (sequence-verified).
    Negative control: E. coli DH5α without any plasmid.

    Induction Protocol

    1. Prepare LB media with salicylic acid at 0, 50, 100, 500, 1 000 μM; 3 replicates per group.
    2. Inoculate overnight cultures at 1:100 into each concentration.
    3. Shake at 37 °C, 200 rpm until OD₆₀₀ reaches 0.4–0.6 (~2–3 h), then continue induction for 2–4 h.
    4. Transfer 200 μL from each group into a 96-well black plate.
    5. Measure fluorescence (Ex 488 nm / Em 511 nm) and OD₆₀₀ using a microplate reader; 3 technical replicates per well.
    Result: Fluorescence analysis confirmed NahR is essential for Psal activation. The reconstructed biosensor achieved a detection limit of 100 μM.

    4. Optimal Host & Detection Time

    I. Optimal Host Screening

    Strain & Plasmid Preparation

    Four E. coli competent cell strains were used: XL10-gold, DH10B, Top10, and DH5α. The recombinant plasmid pUC57-Pr-NahR(E169/C248)-Psal-eGFP was extracted for transformation.

    Transformation & Screening

    1. Transform plasmid into all four strains via heat-shock protocol.
    2. Plate on LA + Kana (50 μg/mL); incubate 37 °C, 12–16 h upside down.
    3. Pick 3 colonies per plate into LB + Kana; shake 37 °C, 200 rpm overnight to obtain 4 engineered strains.

    Salicylic Acid Gradient Induction

    LB media were prepared with salicylic acid at 0, 100, 500, 1 000 μM (3 biological replicates each). All four strains diluted 1:100, incubated at 37 °C, 200 rpm for the same duration.

    Fluorescence Detection

    200 μL from each group loaded into 96-well black plates. Fluorescence (Ex 488 / Em 511 nm) and OD₆₀₀ measured. Relative fluorescence = FL / OD₆₀₀.

    Conclusion: By comparing relative fluorescence across all four strains at different salicylic acid concentrations, E. coli DH5α was selected as the optimal host.

    II. Optimal Detection Time

    Strain Activation

    Optimal host E. coli DH5α/pUC57-Pr-NahR(E169/C248)-Psal-eGFP was cultured overnight in LB + Kana at 37 °C, 200 rpm.

    Time-Course Induction

    LB media with 0, 50, 100, 500, 1 000 μM salicylic acid (3 replicates each). Inoculated 1:100, cultured at 37 °C, 200 rpm. Samples (200 μL) taken at 0, 4, 6, 8, and 10 h post-induction.

    Result: At 100 μM salicylic acid, fluorescence response increased over time, with the most significant increase observed at 10 h. Therefore, 10 h was determined as the optimal detection time.
